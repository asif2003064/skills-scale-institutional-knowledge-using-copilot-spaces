# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather and analyze requirements, facilitate stakeholder interviews, and maintain clear documentation that supports effective decision-making across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into functional specifications
- Facilitate workshops and interviews with stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Maintain requirements traceability matrices
- Support UAT (User Acceptance Testing) planning and execution

### Goals
- Ensure alignment between business objectives and technical solutions
- Reduce requirement ambiguity and rework
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction through clear communication
- Enable data-driven decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Functional specifications and process flow diagrams
- User story refinement sessions with Product and Development teams
- Status updates on requirements validation

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on product vision and prioritization; translate product goals into detailed requirements
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria; validate technical feasibility
- **Project Managers**: Provide requirements documentation to support project planning and risk identification
- **QA Lead**: Define acceptance criteria and support test scenario development
- **UX Designer**: Align on user needs and ensure design solutions meet business requirements

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers craft intuitive and accessible user experiences. They conduct user research, design user journeys, create wireframes and prototypes, and ensure that usability and accessibility objectives are prioritized throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create user personas, journey maps, and wireframes
- Design intuitive interfaces that meet accessibility standards
- Collaborate on product requirements and feature definitions
- Validate design solutions through user feedback and testing
- Maintain design systems and style guides
- Advocate for user needs throughout the development process

### Goals
- Deliver user-friendly, accessible products
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across products
- Balance user needs with business and technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaboration sessions with Product and Development teams

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs and product vision; validate feature priorities through user research
- **Business Analysts**: Collaborate on understanding user and business requirements
- **Developers**: Work together on design implementation; ensure technical feasibility
- **QA Lead**: Define usability test scenarios and acceptance criteria for user experience
- **Agile Coach**: Participate in design sprints and iterative design processes

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads design and oversee quality assurance strategies, ensuring that products meet defined quality standards. They manage test planning, defect triaging, and coordinate testing activities across development and release cycles.

### Responsibilities
- Develop comprehensive test strategies and plans
- Define quality metrics and acceptance criteria
- Coordinate test execution across manual and automated testing
- Manage defect tracking, triaging, and resolution
- Conduct risk assessments for releases
- Mentor QA team members and promote testing best practices
- Collaborate with development on testability and quality improvements
- Coordinate with release management on deployment readiness

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and reliability
- Minimize production defects and customer-impacting issues
- Optimize test coverage and efficiency
- Foster a quality-first culture across teams

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and quality reports
- Defect triage meetings and status updates
- Release readiness assessments
- Quality metrics dashboards and retrospectives

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on testability, bug fixes, and test automation strategies
- **Product Managers**: Align on quality standards and prioritize defect resolution
- **Project Managers**: Provide quality status updates; identify testing-related risks and dependencies
- **Business Analysts**: Validate acceptance criteria and support UAT planning
- **UX Designer**: Coordinate on usability testing and accessibility validation

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches guide teams in adopting and refining agile practices. They facilitate process improvements, support team retrospectives, and foster a culture of continuous learning and collaboration.

### Responsibilities
- Guide teams in agile methodology adoption and refinement
- Facilitate sprint planning, retrospectives, and other ceremonies
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Identify and remove process impediments
- Support teams in continuous improvement initiatives
- Mentor Scrum Masters and team leads
- Promote collaboration and psychological safety
- Measure and track team health and process metrics

### Goals
- Increase team effectiveness and delivery velocity
- Foster continuous improvement and learning culture
- Enhance collaboration and communication across teams
- Enable teams to become self-organizing and high-performing

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of agile ceremonies (standups, retrospectives, planning)
- Process improvement workshops and training sessions
- Team health assessments and maturity reports
- One-on-one coaching conversations with team members

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Support project delivery through process optimization and team coaching
- **Developers**: Coach on engineering practices and collaboration techniques
- **Product Managers**: Facilitate backlog refinement and prioritization practices
- **Executive Sponsor**: Report on team maturity and process improvement initiatives
- **All roles**: Promote cross-functional collaboration and continuous learning

---

## Executive Sponsor

### Role Summary
Executive Sponsors provide strategic oversight and ensure alignment between projects and business objectives. They remove escalated blockers, secure resources, and champion initiatives at the leadership level.

### Responsibilities
- Provide strategic direction and alignment with business goals
- Secure budget, resources, and organizational support
- Remove escalated blockers and organizational impediments
- Make high-level decisions on scope, priority, and trade-offs
- Champion the project across the organization
- Review and approve major milestones and deliverables
- Ensure stakeholder alignment at the executive level
- Support change management and organizational adoption

### Goals
- Ensure project success and strategic business value
- Maintain executive-level stakeholder alignment
- Enable teams to deliver by removing organizational barriers
- Drive organizational change and transformation

### Typical Communication
- Executive steering committee meetings
- Strategic project updates and milestone reviews
- Escalation resolution and decision-making
- Stakeholder briefings and organizational announcements

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Receive status updates; resolve escalated issues and approve key decisions
- **Product Managers**: Align on strategic product direction and business value
- **Agile Coach**: Support organizational transformation and process improvement initiatives
- **All roles**: Provide executive visibility, support, and guidance when needed
- **Business Analysts**: Review business cases and strategic requirements
